# Camera Rules (cross-format)

**The base rule (A-roll & most B-roll):** the camera is either **completely static**
OR has a **slight, natural handheld drift**. Never zoom, dolly, pan, or any other move
— zooms and punch-ins are added in the EDIT (10% jump-cut punches), not in generation.
Write it explicitly: "The camera remains completely static with no movement" or
"The camera introduces a slight, natural handheld drift throughout the shot."

**Format-specific exceptions (each format's SOP states its own):**
- `interview`: handheld run-and-gun energy is the look — "unstable handheld framing,
  slight breathing motion" is allowed; still no zooms or whip-pans in generation.
- `lofi-text`: ambient loops may use extremely slow drift; loops must end near their
  first frame so the loop point doesn't jump.
- `play-pause`: the angled watching-setup shot stays fully static (the overlaid clip
  provides the motion).

**Intonation (voiced clips — Kling):** the voice follows the dialogue punctuation. A
trailing ellipsis reads as a rising, uncertain inflection. For a statement, end with a
firm period and add "delivers the line as a calm, matter-of-fact STATEMENT with a
settled, declarative, falling intonation, NOT a question." Convert mid-line ellipses
to commas/periods unless a hesitant pause is wanted. Quote dialogue exactly as it
should be spoken.
