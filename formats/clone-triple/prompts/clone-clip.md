# Prompt — clone-triple turn clip (`kling3_0`, sound ON, ≤8s)

Start frame = the composite still. ONE clone speaks; the other(s) HOLD their pose,
listening/reacting. Name exactly who speaks and what the others do. SAME voice tag for
all clones (one person); attitude varies. Continuity close.

## Template
> Begin on the composite `<two/three>`-shot from the reference, all clones present and
> consistent. The clone on the `<LEFT/RIGHT/CENTER>` begins speaking `<to the other
> clone and to camera>` in `<voice tag verbatim>`, `<delivery attitude: dry-skeptical /
> energetic-persuasive / calm-wise>`: "`<TURN dialogue, exact words>`". `<Speaking
> clone's gesture.>` The `<other clone(s)>` stay still, `<their holding reaction, e.g.
> listening with a doubtful eyebrow / nodding slowly>`. All faces, wardrobe, lighting,
> and the background stay completely consistent — a seamless single take.

Keep ≤8s. If the non-speaking clone drifts (face/wardrobe changes), re-roll; consider
splitting a long turn.

## Worked example (CLONE1 skeptic speaks)
> Begin on the composite two-shot from the reference, both clones present and
> consistent. The clone on the LEFT begins speaking across to the right clone in a dry,
> deadpan `<voice tag verbatim>`: "That's literally what they all say." She gives a
> flat, unimpressed look and a small hand-wave. The clone on the RIGHT stays still,
> holding her eager lean-in, a hopeful raised eyebrow. Both faces, wardrobe, lighting,
> and background stay completely consistent — a seamless single take.
